Introducing Cloud Computing

A look at computing: past, present, and future.

Pretend you just work up; many questions come to your mind: "What time is it; am I late for work"; "Whta's the weather like?"; "Wonder if my stocks are going to go back up today otherwise I can kiss that car/trip/dinner goodbye".

So, you grab your phone, iPad, whatever device and start browsing. You actually don't really have to read a newspaper or turn on the TV or even have to set an alarm clock; just have to have a "smart" device.

These are examples of Cloud Computing (Level 1: Unidirectional) where the data is pushed for free, for anyone, without any security. Of course when anything is free, you must exercise "Buyer Beware": don't trust everything you read or see or hear. Someone "out there" is sending information which available to anyone, anytime, anywhere.
